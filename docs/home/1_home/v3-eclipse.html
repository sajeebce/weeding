Yes, absolutely. There is no requirement to be a US citizen or resident to form a US LLC. Non-US residents can legally own and operate a US LLC. This is one of the main advantages of the US business structure — it's fully accessible to international entrepreneurs from any country.
For most international entrepreneurs, Wyoming and Delaware are the most popular choices. Wyoming offers no state income tax, low annual fees ($60/year), and the strongest privacy protections. Delaware is preferred if you plan to raise venture capital or do significant business in the US. We'll help you choose the right state based on your specific situation.
An EIN (Employer Identification Number) is your US business Tax ID number, assigned by the IRS. It's required to open a US business bank account, hire employees, file US taxes, set up payment processors like Stripe and PayPal, and apply for business credit. As a non-US resident, obtaining an EIN requires specific paperwork — we handle this entire process for you.
For single-member LLCs owned by non-US residents with no US-based operations or customers, there is generally no US income tax liability. The LLC is treated as a "disregarded entity" for US tax purposes. However, you must file an annual informational return (Form 5472). We strongly recommend consulting a US CPA familiar with international tax for your specific situation. Note: LLCPad is not a law firm and this is not legal or tax advice.
Processing times vary by state. With our Standard and Premium packages, Wyoming LLCs are typically approved within 24-48 hours. Delaware takes 3-5 business days for standard filing, or 24 hours with expedited processing. EIN processing takes an additional 1-2 business days for non-US residents. We'll provide real-time status updates throughout the entire process.
