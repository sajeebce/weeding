FAQ

Common questions, answered

Everything you need to know about forming and running a US LLC as an international entrepreneur.

Yes, absolutely. US law places no citizenship or residency requirement on LLC ownership. Non-US residents from any country can legally own and operate a US LLC. This is one of the main reasons LLCs are so popular with international entrepreneurs.
For most international entrepreneurs running online businesses, Wyoming is the best choice — no state income tax, the lowest annual fees ($60/year), and the strongest privacy protections. Delaware is preferred if you're raising venture capital. We'll advise you during consultation based on your specific situation.
An EIN (Employer Identification Number) is your LLC's federal tax ID. Without it, you cannot open a US bank account, set up Stripe or PayPal, apply for an Amazon seller account, or file US taxes. Non-US residents need to apply via specific IRS procedures — we handle this entire process for you.
Generally, single-member LLCs owned by non-US residents with no US-source income have no US income tax liability. However, you must file Form 5472 annually (informational return). Tax obligations vary based on your country, business type, and income sources. Always consult a qualified international tax advisor — this is not tax advice.
Wyoming LLCs are typically approved in 24-48 hours with our service. Delaware takes 3-5 business days for standard filing. EIN processing takes 1-2 additional business days for non-US residents. With our Premium package, you get priority processing and can have your full LLC + EIN delivered within 48 hours.
We offer a 30-day money-back guarantee on all service fees. If we make an error in your filing, we fix it at no additional cost. If your LLC is rejected due to our mistake, we refund our service fee in full. Note: state government filing fees are paid directly to the government and are non-refundable.
