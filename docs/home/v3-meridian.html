Yes, completely. US law allows foreign nationals to own and operate LLCs. There is no citizenship or residency requirement. This is exactly why LLCs are so popular among international entrepreneurs — you get full access to the US market, banking, and payment processors.
For most international entrepreneurs running online businesses (e-commerce, freelancing, SaaS), Wyoming is the best choice — no state income tax, low annual fee ($60/year), and strong privacy protections. Delaware is better if you plan to raise venture capital. We'll help you choose based on your goals during intake.
An EIN (Employer Identification Number) is your US business Tax ID. You need it to open a US bank account, set up Stripe/PayPal, hire employees, file US taxes, and apply for an Amazon seller account. Without it, your LLC is nearly non-functional. We handle the entire IRS application process for you.
Generally, a single-member LLC owned by a non-US resident with no US-source income has no US income tax liability. However, you must file Form 5472 annually (informational only). Tax laws are complex and depend on your country, business type, and income sources. We recommend consulting a US-qualified international tax advisor. Note: this is not legal or tax advice.
